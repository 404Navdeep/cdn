# Preface

## Why I Wrote This Story

I wrote Room 412 because I've always been interested in creepy stories. The kind of stories that make you think about weird things that don't make sense.

I was thinking about how there are a lot of old motels on the side of highways that nobody really goes to anymore. Places that look abandoned and dangerous. I wondered what it would be like to get trapped in one of those places. What if the motel itself was weird and strange? What if it wasn't a normal place at all?

The scariest stories are the ones where you can't explain what's happening. Where nothing makes sense and you don't know what to believe. That's what I wanted to write about.

Marcus Webb is just a regular person driving to San Diego. He's not looking for trouble. He just needs gas and a place to sleep. But sometimes the worst things happen to normal people when they're in the wrong place at the wrong time.

I also liked the idea that nobody would believe the story afterwards. Nobody would believe that the motel was real. That's the scary part—when something terrible happens and nobody believes you.

I hope you enjoy reading Room 412. I hope it creeps you out a little bit. And I hope you think about it for a while after you finish.
