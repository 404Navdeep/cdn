# Room 412
## A Short Story

---

## Chapter 1: Wrong Turn

It was raining really hard when I was driving to San Diego.

My name is Marcus and I was in my Honda Civic. My gas tank was almost empty. The GPS stopped working like twenty minutes ago because there was no signal. The rain was so bad I could barely see the road.

I think I was on Highway 43 or something. I'm not really sure. I missed the exit I was supposed to take like forty minutes ago and ended up on this weird road that nobody really goes on. It looked like the kind of place where people just disappear and nobody finds them.

My phone was dead. My coffee was cold. And I really needed to use the bathroom.

Then I saw the motel.

It said HW-43 MOTEL on the sign. The sign was blinking pink and green. The building looked really old and dirty. It was kind of yellowish and the paint was peeling everywhere. There was only one floor that I could see.

In the parking lot there was a car that looked like it hadn't been driven in years. It was all rusty and the tires were flat. There was a paper on the windshield that said "Missing Person" on it.

I was almost out of gas so I had to stop there.

The guy working at the front desk didn't really care about anything. He was old and he didn't ask me for ID or anything. He just gave me a key. The key had a green thing on it that said **412**.

"The stairs are around the side," he said. He didn't even look at me.

I thought that was weird because the building only had one floor. Where would the stairs be? But I was tired and I really needed a bathroom so I didn't ask him about it.

I just took the key and left.

---

## Chapter 2: The Room That Was Already There

The stairs actually existed. I found them on the side of the building like the guy said. But that didn't make sense because I couldn't see them from outside.

The hallway was really weird. It was way longer than it should have been. The lights were those old fluorescent ones that buzz and flicker. The numbers on the doors kept changing. I saw 410, then 411, then 412, then 412 again, then 411 again. It was like the numbers couldn't decide what they were.

The hallway smelled bad. Like old metal and something rotten.

Room 412 was at the very end. Of course it was. The door was already open a little bit. And I could hear something moving inside. Like something shifting around.

When I opened the door, the room looked normal. There were two beds. A TV that looked really old. A bathroom. Orange curtains on the window.

But my suitcase was on the bed.

It was the exact same blue duffel bag I packed three days ago. The zipper was broken just like I remembered. I was standing there staring at it and thinking: *This is not real. This is not happening. I'm awake right now.*

"Okay," I said out loud to the empty room. "This is messed up."

I went to the bathroom and looked in the mirror. Someone had written something on it with the shower steam. It said:

**YOU ALREADY STAYED**

The letters were shaky and weird looking.

I was freaking out. The TV turned on by itself. It was just showing static and weird shadow shapes that looked like they could be people. The sound was off but the speakers were making this hissing noise.

The window showed a parking lot that was different from the one outside. The cars were different. The sky was darker like it had been raining for a really long time.

I heard footsteps in the hallway. They were getting closer and closer.

I opened the door and looked out. The hallway was empty. Just the flickering lights and the bad smell.

But I could still hear the footsteps.

---

## Chapter 3: The People Who Stayed Here

I found this book in the nightstand drawer. It looked like a guest book or something. It had dates going back to like 1983. All the entries were for Room 412. And a lot of the names had lines through them like they were crossed out.

There was a newspaper clipping taped to one of the pages. It said "LOCAL MAN MISSING AFTER ROADSIDE STOP" from 2011. The picture showed a guy named Robert Chen. He was 34 years old and was last seen at the HW-43 Motel.

When I looked at the bedframe, someone had carved words into the wood. It said **SARAH—DAY 47**.

In the closet there was more carved words: **HELP ME**.

The bathroom had more messages carved into the tile. They said things like **CAN'T LEAVE** and **ROOM CHANGES** and **IT WEARS THEIR FACES**.

I was really scared now. I tried to leave the room.

The hallway seemed to get longer and longer. I walked and walked and counted my steps. Ten, twenty, thirty. The hallway just kept going. The doors all looked the same and when I looked back, Room 412 was right there with the door open.

That's when I saw him.

There was a man at the end of the hallway. He looked exactly like me. Same shirt. Same scared face. He was holding my suitcase.

"Did you find the ledger?" he asked me.

"What? Who are you?" I asked.

"I'm what happens on Day 48," he said. "You're going to stay here a long time, Marcus. I've been here a long time. So did the people before me."

I started running. I ran back to the room and locked the door but the lock didn't work. I went to the window and jumped out even though it looked really high.

I was falling and falling and I was thinking: *At least I tried to get out.*

## Chapter 4: The Motel That Doesn't Exist

**From the San Diego Police Report**
**Date: March 14, 2026**

To Marcus Webb's Family,

We looked for the motel that your son said he stayed at. We could not find it. There is no motel on Highway 43. We looked everywhere. There are no records of any motel like that. There are no buildings there. There is no car like he described.

We think maybe he left the area on his own. People sometimes do that when they get stressed.

We could not find any proof that the motel exists.

We are sorry we could not help.

Police Detective James Morrison

---

**Email to Marcus's Mom**
**April 2, 2026**

Dear Mrs. Webb,

I understand you are upset. But I cannot look for a motel that does not exist on any records. We have looked everywhere. There is nothing there.

Your son's phone was found at a rest stop 30 miles away. His blue suitcase was found in a donation box at Goodwill in Phoenix three weeks later. It had his stuff in it.

But without a body or proof that a crime happened, we cannot keep looking.

I am sorry.

Detective James Morrison

---

**From Marcus's Diary**
**The morning he left - March 10, 2026**

I had the weirdest dream last night. I was at some motel and my suitcase was already there when I arrived. The hallway kept getting longer. There was a person who looked like me. It felt so real. It felt like I had been there before.

What a strange dream.

Anyway, I'm leaving for my road trip to San Diego now. I'll be on the road all day.

---

**The Truth**

The motel is still there on Highway 43. But nobody remembers it being built. The guy at the desk is still waiting. The parking lot still has the rusty car with the missing person poster.

Room 412 is always empty. Until someone needs it.

In the guest book, there is a new name written:

**MARCUS WEBB - DAY 1**

The question is not how long he will stay. The question is how many times he will stay.

The motel remembers everything.
