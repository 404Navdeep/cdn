# What is Cloud and VPS? The New Way of Compute

## Introduction

So okay, like, back in the day computers were just these huge machines that sat on your desk, right? They cost a lot of money and took up a bunch of space. And like, if you needed more storage or wanted your computer to be faster, you basically had to buy a completely new one. Which was like, super annoying and expensive.

Everyone kept their stuff on their own computers. If you wanted to share something with someone, you'd have to use a USB drive or like, email them the file or whatever. Schools had those computer labs with a ton of computers that were always slow and it was kind of a mess.

Then the internet became like, actually good. And people realized something: why do we even need computers at home if we can just use computers that are like, somewhere else on the internet? That's where cloud computing and VPS come in. Basically they're changing how we use computers because they're easier, they actually work way better, and they're way cheaper.

## What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing basically means like, you use computers and storage that are on the internet instead of like, your own computer. So basically you're not doing the work, some other computer somewhere is doing it for you.

You probably already use this and don't even know it, which is kind of funny. Like Google Drive, you save your documents up there and you can like, see them from anywhere. When you play online games, you're not downloading the whole game to your computer, it's just like, playing from somewhere else. Same with Netflix and Spotify. You just like, watch or listen and everything is stored on their computers somewhere.

Why cloud computing is actually pretty cool:

- You can use it from anywhere. Your room, school, coffee shop, whatever. Doesn't matter.
- You don't have to like, spend tons of money on your own computer.
- If you need more power, you can just like, get it. It's pretty easy.

Companies love this because like, they save so much money. They don't have to buy all these expensive machines and hire people to fix them all the time. They just like, pay someone else to handle it. And it's faster too, you don't have to wait like weeks for stuff to arrive. Oh and if something goes wrong, there's usually a backup so you don't lose everything.

## Types of Cloud Services

Okay so like, there are basically three types of cloud services:

**Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)** - So this is like renting computers online. You get a computer, storage, and like, internet stuff. You can literally do whatever you want with it. Amazon Web Services is like, the big one that everyone uses.

**Platform as a Service (PaaS)** - This one is like, for people who make apps and stuff. It gives you tools to make things without like, worrying about all the boring computer parts. It's like having someone help you do the annoying stuff.

**Software as a Service (SaaS)** - These are like, programs you use on the internet. Gmail, Google Docs, Slack, all that stuff. You just like, log in and use them. You don't have to download anything or worry about updates or whatever.

## What is a VPS?

Okay so VPS stands for Virtual Private Server. Basically it's like, a fake computer that lives inside a real computer. So like, one really big powerful computer can be like, split up into smaller computers, and each one like, works as its own machine. It's kind of confusing but it makes sense if you think about it.

So like, what's the difference between this and normal hosting? Well with regular hosting, like a bunch of websites share the same computer. It's like living in an apartment where everyone has to share the wifi and if one person uses it too much, everyone else is like, really slow. With a VPS, you get your own space basically. You have like, guaranteed power and speed, and if someone else's website gets like, a ton of traffic, it doesn't slow down your stuff.

A VPS is like, really good if you want to make a website or app that's like, pretty powerful, but you don't want to like, buy an actual computer and pay someone to take care of it. You get a lot of control, you can install whatever software you want, and it costs way less than like, buying your own server. So yeah it's basically like, the middle ground between shared hosting and owning your own computer.

## Cloud vs VPS: What's the Difference?

Okay so like, both cloud and VPS use computers on the internet, right? And like, both let you access your stuff from anywhere. But like, they're not the same thing. They're kind of different.

Cloud computing can like, grow really easily and really fast. If you like, suddenly need way more power, you can get it in like five minutes. It's like, perfect if you're not sure how much power you'll need or if you're like, a company that's growing super fast.

VPS is like, usually cheaper and like, you know exactly what you're getting. Your stuff is like, your own space basically. It doesn't like, change a lot, which is good if you like, want to know what you're paying for every month.

So like, which one is better? Honestly it like, depends on what you need. If you're like, a big company and you need like, a ton of power and you don't know how much, cloud is probably better. If you're like, a small person or you have a small website and you like, need something that works the same every day, VPS is probably better. You know?
